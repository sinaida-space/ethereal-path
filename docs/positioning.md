# Ethereal Path — B2B Positioning (v1.2 draft)

*Working draft, 2026-07. One page for deciding who we call and what we say. Not marketing copy.*

## The one-liner

**The screen-break directive, made beautiful.** A 5-minute verified movement
ritual for screen-bound teams — the camera confirms the stretches happened,
and nothing ever leaves the device.

## The regulatory hook

EU Directive 90/270/EEC (display screen equipment), Article 7: every EU
employer must plan screen work to be "periodically interrupted by breaks or
changes of activity." National transpositions vary (NL caps consecutive
screen work at 2 hours; DE/CZ have analogous duty-of-care rules). Most
companies satisfy this with a policy PDF nobody reads. Ethereal Path is the
version employees actually do — and the EHS file gets a real artifact.

## Category

MSK (musculoskeletal) micro-prevention for screen workers.
- Not meditation (Headspace/Calm own it; soft ROI).
- Not fitness (Gympass/Wellhub own it; requires leaving the desk).
- MSK complaints are a top-2 ergonomic cost driver in office populations and
  a line item HR already owns.

## The moat (say it first, always)

**Verification without surveillance.** On-device pose estimation: no video
recorded, no video transmitted, no individual reporting — architecturally,
not as a policy promise. GDPR-friendly, works-council-friendly. Camera-based
wellness competitors cannot say this. In Europe this is the buying argument.

## Who to offer it to (in order)

1. **Dev & design studios, 20–200 seats, NL/CZ/DE.** Longest sitting hours,
   care about beautiful tools, short buying chains (founder or head of people
   decides). Warm entry: Sinaida's Amsterdam network + Prague community.
2. **EHS / ergonomics consultancies.** They already sell DSE assessments;
   Ethereal Path is a deliverable they can resell ("here's your break
   program"). Channel, not direct.
3. **Coworking operators (secondary).** Amenity play: branded deck on the
   member portal. Low ACV, but visible.
4. *(later)* **Occupational health insurers** — prevention subsidy programs
   (e.g. DE §20 SGB V prevention budgets) once there's usage data.

## The offer ladder

| Step | What | Price hypothesis |
|---|---|---|
| Free | Personal ritual at the public URL, forever free | — |
| Pilot | 4 weeks, one team (≤30 seats), team deck + kickoff call + end report (aggregate participation only) | €0–500, credited on conversion |
| Team | Per-seat/month: Slack/Teams nudges, deck selection, shared team night-sky, DSE-compliance summary for the EHS file | €3–5 / seat / mo |
| Studio+ | Custom deck (their exercises, their palette), branded end-card | setup fee €1–2k |

Anti-metric commitment in every contract: **no individual data, ever.**
Aggregate counts only (sessions completed per team per week). This is the
privacy moat expressed as a product decision.

## Founder story (why buyers believe her)

Biomedical engineer (Bauman, cancer-screening algorithms) × GE-trained
project manager × decade of cultural-sector creative direction. In a category
full of vibes, she can cite the directive, read the MSK literature, and
design the light. Pitch as "medically literate art," not wellness.

## 90-day plan

1. **Weeks 1–2:** landing page = the app itself + a `/teams` section (copy
   from this doc). Demo video (screen capture, 60s, no voiceover).
2. **Weeks 3–6:** 10 warm outreaches (Amsterdam/Prague studios) offering the
   pilot. Goal: 3 pilots. Use /outreach for personalized one-pagers once v1.2
   screenshots exist.
3. **Weeks 7–12:** run pilots, collect aggregate usage + testimonial quotes,
   convert 1–2 to paid. Learn which deck lengths and nudge cadences stick.

## Objection handling

- *"Employees won't turn on a camera."* — Camera is optional; every exercise
  has a no-camera mode. And nothing leaves the device — show the network tab.
- *"We already have Headspace."* — Headspace doesn't move a single muscle in
  the neck. This is the physical counterpart, 5 minutes, no app install.
- *"Prove people use it."* — Pilot report: aggregate sessions/week. That's
  the only number that exists, by design.
- *"Why not free forever?"* — The personal version is. Teams pay for
  orchestration (nudges, decks, the shared sky) and the compliance artifact.
